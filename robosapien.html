"Robosapien Explored"




The “Workshop On Robotics” was organized, promoted and framed by “The Parikalan Society” for the students of Computer Science Department in “Seminar Room” on “17th Oct 2015”.
The “Center of Robotics and Artificial Intelligence, Ramanujan College, Delhi University” that is working on Autonomous Robotics and Robots actively, took the workshop and made it worth a memorable workshop. In the short span of 4 hours The Mentor and Convenor “Dr. Nikhil Kumar Rajput” with his spectacular team and their “FireBird V” Robots, incredibly managed to take the selected 5 teams and the other enthusiastic participants from very basics of Robotics to the extent of coding, movements and sensing all through fantasy world of artificial intelligence.
The participants were given hands on training on “Fire Bird Robot” and were also given the “Certificate Of Participation” issued by the “Center of Robotics and AI, Ramanujan College” itself. The participants were really encouraged to be a part of ever innovative, creative, and very interesting field of “Robotics”.
Dr. Nikhil specially focused on firstly knowing the circuit and your robot, to know its ports, limits, sensors, parts, placements, abilities, functionalities and obviously working. Then secondly Circuit Idea, Motors Control, Interfacing Motors, Sensors Interfacing, Master And Slave IC architecture, Micro Controllers were discussed one by one. The software AVRStudio was introduced for using to code algorithms in Objective C and uploading manually to Robots for managing the functionalities.
It was really impressive that the selected teams were able to understand the algorithms, the high end FireBird Robot fitted with Light and IR sensors, and were able to program the robot to follow specified paths, the White line following robot, to control the beeping signals and all other functionalities.
The credit for such a well managed, practical as well as theoretical, promising workshop goes to the whole team of Parikalan who worked very hard for a week to make this workshop a success on the basis of quality and quantity both.
