Tech-Qwhiz



Parikalan, the CS society of PGDAV College organized an interdepartmental quiz competition on October 29, 2015. The competition was open to all students under CS department. Since prior registrations were already done, a huge turnout was expected, and so did happened. The enthusiastic students of B.Tech and B.Sc. Computer Science and B.Sc. Mathematical Science participated in full swing.
The Quiz coordinator,Shivam Ahuja, started off the event by welcoming the participants and reading out the rules for the upcoming rounds. The program kicked off with a preliminary round wherein each participants was given a set of questions of MCQs and fill in the blank type to be solved within a stipulated time. The first round not only test the knowledge, but also how fast you can utilize your knowledge to bring out the best result. After the hardship of the first round, the quiz team shortlisted the top 11 participants who qualified for the next CROSSWORD round. All selected participants were given a technical crossword to solve which tested the extent of technical knowledge our building Engineers and future developer have. On the basis of the crosswords, we had out 5 finalists selected for the final and the most thrilling Buzzer round.
The stage was set, our quiz master Shivam was on Podium, the Score and time keeper Hritik was all set too and the finalists were ready for the final battle. The Buzzer round not only demanded knowledge in technical field, but also tested the speed, accuracy and confidence of the participant. After the intense rounds of buzzer, it went all the way down to the final decider question. The final question was up on the slide. It was silence all around, till the loud buzzer broke the silence. “It’s the correct answer”, the quiz master confirmed the audience and we had our winner amongst us.
It was Vishal Singh – 1st Year Computer Science student who clinched the quiz competition.
The competition ended with quite satisfied participants and coordinators. The participants and coordinators. The participants played their heart out and the coordinators left no stone unturned to make the event a huge success.
